Cued Music · private beta

The whole world of music, tuned to your taste — for every moment.

Cued is a music guide for Apple Music. Tell it the moment — a morning emo run, cooking jazz, the gym — and it answers five songs at a time: favorites you forgot you loved, next to music you've never met.

You liked five hundred albums. You hear the same forty songs.

Streaming apps lean on what you played recently, so last month's habits pick next month's music. The library you spent years building goes quiet, and discovery drifts toward whatever everyone else is playing.

Cued starts from everything you've ever loved and lets the moment decide what surfaces. Here's the whole journey.

I Define your taste

Your library is the map.

Connect Apple Music and everything you've already liked becomes the raw material — years of your own choices, not a stranger's charts. Cued reads what you love, and just as carefully, what you've buried: the record you played to death in 2019 and haven't touched since. That's where it starts digging.

II Cue the moment

Name any occasion. It becomes a place you can return to.

Type it the way you'd say it out loud: morning emo run cooking jazz sunday reset punjabi gym. Cued answers with a pack — five songs that fit the moment and you. Presets when you don't feel like typing. Made-up words welcome; it works out what you meant with you, a pack at a time.

Every vibe you name is saved. Your gym tomorrow knows everything your gym learned today.

III Explore the world

Radio from any song. Discovery that actually fits.

Caught on one song you can't let go of? Start a radio from it. The song plays first, then Cued follows the thread — same blood, tuned by your taste, digging past the obvious hits into the deep cuts. Mid-pack, ask for more like this and it leans in.

New artists show up because they sit next to what you already love. Keep what lands, skip what doesn't — every choice sharpens the next five songs.

IV No repeats

It remembers what it played you. So it never loops.

Cued keeps track of every song it has served you — across packs, across days. Tomorrow's gym doesn't replay yesterday's. And when you return to a vibe, it doesn't start over; it picks up where you left off, a little sharper than you left it.

Your gym ends up sounding like nobody else's, because it was learned from nobody else.
